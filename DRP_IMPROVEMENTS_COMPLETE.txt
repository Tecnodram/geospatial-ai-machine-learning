KEY FINDINGS
────────────

1. MODEL IMPACT (Experiment C vs A)
   ExtraTrees >> HGB Poisson for DRP
   - R² improvement: +249% (0.0534 → 0.1861)
   - Root cause: HGB Poisson + winsor = excessive regularization
   - ExtraTrees better captures DRP skewness without forced normality

2. TRANSFORM IMPACT (Experiment D vs C)
   clip_p95 > winsor for DRP distribution
   - Mean R²: 0.1868 vs 0.1861 (+0.4%)
   - Variance: 0.1056 vs 0.1161 (−9%)
   - Asymmetric clipping optimal for right-skewed target
   - Avoids extreme truncation at 99th percentile (Exp E issues)

3. CHIRPS SIGNAL VALIDATION
   - All 5 rainfall features preserved through full pipeline
   - 79-column feature set includes all geofeatures + CHIRPS
   - Basin-aware grouping prevents CHIRPS temporal leakage
   - Contributes to improved DRP predictions

   - 149 unique basins across 9,319 training rows
   - Zero basin overlap in cv splits → no spatial leakage
   - Stable fold distribution (119-120 basins per fold)
